Haleem

Haleem is a popular dish in Pakistan. Haleem is made with mutton, lentils, and wheat. It is a hearty and flavorful dish that is often enjoyed during the winter months.
Haleem is also a high calorie dish which gives instant energy as it contains slow-digesting and fast-burning ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 tsp lamb (boneless)
  • 150 gms moong dal
  • 50 gms dal chana
  • 100 gms wheat (broken)
  • 500 ml desi ghee
  • 30 gms green chilly paste
  • 10 gms turmeric powder
  • 6 gms mace
  • 10 gms green cardamom
  • 50 gms garlic paste
  • 50 gms ginger, grated
  • 150 gms onion, fried
  • 3-4 litre lamb stock

Method

  1. Soak the broken wheat and lentils (urad dal, yellow moong dal, chana dal, and toor dal) separately for at least 2 hours.

  2. Cooking the Mutton and Lentil Mixture. In a large pot, add the mutton, soaked lentils (drained), ginger paste, garlic paste,
    red chilli powder, turmeric, salt, and enough water to cover everything.

  3. Cook over medium heat until the mutton is tender and falling apart, and the lentils have turned into a paste-like consistency,
    stirring occasionally. This may take around 2-3 hours.

  4. Shredding the Mutton Remove the mutton from the pot and shred it with a fork or chop it finely. Keep it aside.

  5. Frying the Onions In a pan, heat ghee and fry the onions until they turn golden brown.

  6. Adding the Shredded Mutton Add the shredded mutton, garam masala, and pepper to the pan with the fried onions.
    Fry for a few minutes.

  7. Combining the Mutton and Lentil Mixture Add the cooked lentil mixture to the pan with the mutton and stir well.

  8. Adding the Broken Wheat and Other Ingredients. Add the soaked broken wheat to the pan along with 6-8 cups of water,
    or enough to cover everything. Stir well.

  9. Add the green chillies, cashews, and mint to the pan. Mix everything well and let it simmer for 1-2 hours over low heat,
    stirring occasionally, until the haleem reaches a porridge-like consistency.

  10. Serving the Haleem Serve hot, garnished with chopped coriander leaves and more fried onions, if desired.
